The famous Soviet writer Mikhail Sholokhov (1905-1984) won the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1965. He is the author of major works such as: "Stories of the East River" (collection of short stories, 1925), "Rivers of the East" Peaceful Winter" (novel, 1928-1940), "The Wasteland" (novel, 1932-1960), "They Fight for the Fatherland" (novel, 1942), "Hate Science" ( collection of short stories, 1942), "The Fate of a Man" (medium stories, 1956-1957).
